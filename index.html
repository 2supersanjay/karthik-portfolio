Featured Project: Propellant Management Device (PMD)

Propellant Management Device (PMD) — CAD Reconstruction & CFD Analysis

I designed and analyzed a Propellant Management Device (PMD) in SolidWorks based on a replicated research study focused on cryogenic propellant handling in microgravity. PMDs are critical spacecraft components that:

  • Enable reliable cryogenic fuel transfer in microgravity by leveraging surface‑tension‑dominated fluid behavior
  • Prevent ingestion of residual gas, which forms easily due to the extremely low boiling points of cryogenic fuels
  • Guide and separate liquid propellant toward the outlet, ensuring stable feed‑system performance for engines and thrusters

For this project, I:

  • Recreated the full PMD geometry in SolidWorks, including the spherical tank, perforated vane, and internal baffle structures
  • Prepared the CAD for CFD using the watertight geometry feature
  • Ran a Volume of Fluid (VOF) multiphase simulation in ANSYS Fluent to analyze surface-tension dynamics and propellant acquisition performance
  • Validated the results against trends from the replicated study, focusing on capillary behavior

This project demonstrates proficiency in CAD modeling, multiphase CFD, and microgravity propellant system analysis, relevant to propulsion, cryogenic systems, and spacecraft fluid‑management design.
